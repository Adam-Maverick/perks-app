## Executive Summary

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Nigerian employees face a compounding financial crisis:

**Root Cause (Five Whys Analysis):**
1. **Why are employees financially stressed?** â†’ Nigeria's inflation is reducing real incomes
2. **Why can't they save?** â†’ Salaries remain stagnant while cost of living rises
3. **Why don't employers help?** â†’ Most companies offer no benefits beyond salary and basic health insurance
4. **Why don't employees access discounts/deals?** â†’ Fear of scams and fake products from unverified emerging brands creates a trust barrier
5. **Why do employers lack tools?** â†’ They need zero-cost retention solutions to compete for talent in a tough economic climate

**The ROOT ISSUE:** Trust deficit + affordability gap + lack of employer-sponsored financial wellness = employees stuck in financial stress with no viable solution.

### Problem Impact

**For Employees:**
- Limited disposable income with inflation at double digits
- Risk exposure when trying to save through unverified discount platforms
- "Can't afford even with discount" problem for essential purchases
- Time waste searching fragmented discount sources
- No employer support for financial wellness

**For Employers:**
- Talent retention challenges in competitive market
- No cost-effective employee benefits beyond health insurance
- Missing tax optimization opportunities (new 2025 Tax Act incentives)
- Poor employee engagement and productivity from financial stress

**For Merchants:**
- Difficulty reaching employed, verified customer segment
- Trust barriers for emerging brands
- High customer acquisition costs

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

**Discount Platforms (Groupon, deals.com.ng):**
- No financing options (affordability gap remains)
- No trust layer (vetting/escrow)
- No employer integration
- Generic, not workplace-focused

**BNPL Platforms (Credpal, Carbon):**
- No curated verified marketplace
- No employer benefits/stipend features
- Higher default risk without employer integration

**International Players (Amazon, Jumia):**
- Not localized for Nigerian trust concerns
- Expensive for low-income earners
- No employer partnership model

**Competitors (Motherboard/roHealth):**
- Strong on HMO/health benefits but weak on financial wellness
- No BNPL or escrow features
- App stability issues per user reviews
- "Benefits Administrator" positioning vs. "Financial Wellness" platform

**Stipends' Competitive Moat:** Employer partnerships (B2B2C model) + Trust layer (vetting + escrow) + Local market focus + Tax optimization angle + **Anti-debt mission** that competitors cannot easily replicate.

### Proposed Solution

Stipends is a **full-stack financial wellness ecosystem** that combines:

**1. Verified Merchant Marketplace**
- Aggregated discounts from established and emerging brands
- Multi-level vetting process (CAC registration, bank verification, quality checks)
- Trust badges and social proof ("X employees saved â‚¦Y")
- Hyper-local merchant density strategy (10-15 merchants per business district)

**2. Employer Stipend Funding**
- Monthly/quarterly allowances funded by employers into employee accounts
- Zero-cost base platform for employers
- Optional funding feature for competitive advantage
- Tax-efficient under Nigeria Tax Act 2025 (50% additional deduction) â€” *pending tax expert validation*

**3. Escrow Protection System**
- **Auto-applied to first-time purchases** from any merchant (trust-building for employees)
- Criteria-based escrow triggers:
  - All first purchases with a new merchant
  - Optional merchant opt-in for additional trust signal
  - High-value transactions (e.g., >â‚¦20k) based on fraud risk scoring
- Funds held until employee confirms satisfaction
- Auto-release after 14 days with notification reminders
- Evidence-based dispute resolution
- Merchant protection policy to prevent abuse
- **Phase 2 refinement**: Limit escrow to emerging/new merchants only after trust established

**4. Seamless Payroll Integration**
- SSO with company email for minimal friction
- HR dashboard for employee management
- White-glove onboarding with ready-made communication templates  
- **Phase 2**: Automated BNPL deductions from monthly salary (when BNPL launches)

**5. Email Marketing + Full Employee App**
- **Email channel**: Weekly curated deals (1-2 offers) sent to company inbox for discovery
- **Employee PWA (Progressive Web App)**: Full marketplace browsing, checkout, savings dashboard, transaction history
- Employer-endorsed communications for trust signal
- Deep links from email to specific deals in app
- Low-bandwidth optimization for Nigerian network infrastructure



### Key Differentiators

   - Platform leverages Nigeria Tax Act 2025: employers get 150% tax deductibility on employee welfare spending

   - Merchants: Access to verified employed customer segment + bulk acquisition

4. **Enterprise-Grade Integration**
   - Payroll API connections (unlike consumer-only BNPL apps)

5. **Financial Wellness Positioning**
   - Not just "discounts" but comprehensive financial enablement
   - Savings tracking + Budgeting insights + Employer-funded stipends
   - Cumulative impact dashboard and gamification
   - **Anti-debt mission**: Focus on smart spending and employer support, not lending

**Frontend:**
- **Next.js 14+ (React Framework)** for B2C employee portal
  - Server-Side Rendering (SSR) for SEO and performance
  - Progressive Web App (PWA) for mobile-first, low-bandwidth accessibility
  - TypeScript for type safety
- **React Admin Dashboard** for employer/merchant portals

**Backend:**
- **Node.js + NestJS (TypeScript)** for API services
  - Allows shared types with frontend (development speed)
  - Microservices-ready architecture for future scale
  - **Alternative:** Go for high-performance transaction ledger (migrate hot paths later)

**Database:**
- **PostgreSQL** for core transactional data (ACID compliance non-negotiable for fintech)
  - User accounts, transactions, merchant data, stipend balances
- **Redis** for caching (deal feeds, session management, rate limiting)

**Payment Infrastructure:**
- **Paystack** as primary payment gateway (Nigeria market leader)
  - Transaction Splits API for marketplace commission automation
  - Escrow service via partner (AtaraPay) or manual hold-and-release
  - PCI-DSS compliant (offloads security burden)
  - Excellent developer experience and documentation

**BNPL Infrastructure (Phase 2-3 Build):**
- **Proprietary Credit Engine:**
  - Custom scoring algorithm using payroll data + employment tenure
  - Integration with credit bureaus (CRC, FirstCentral) for negative checks
- **Loan Ledger System:**
  - Double-entry accounting for loan disbursements and repayments
  - Automated interest calculation and penalty logic (if applicable)
- **Repayment Automation:**
  - Direct payroll deduction integration (API or file-based)
  - Backup repayment method (tokenized card via Paystack)

**Authentication & Security:**
- **OAuth 2.0 / SAML SSO** for enterprise employee login (work email verification)
- **JWT tokens** for session management
- **2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)** mandatory for employer admin accounts
- **End-to-end encryption** for sensitive data (BVN, NIN, salary info)
- **Payment tokenization** via Paystack (PCI-DSS compliance)

**Infrastructure:**
- **Google Cloud Platform (GCP)** or **AWS** for hosting
  - Cloud Run / ECS for containerized services
  - Cloud SQL / RDS for managed PostgreSQL
  - Cloud Storage / S3 for receipts and documents
- **Vercel** for Next.js frontend deployment (optimized for Next.js)
- **CDN:** Cloudflare for caching and DDoS protection

**DevOps:**
- **CI/CD:** GitHub Actions for automated testing and deployment
- **Monitoring:** Sentry (error tracking), Datadog/New Relic (APM), LogRocket (session replay)
- **Alerting:** PagerDuty for critical payment/escrow failures

### Payroll Integration Strategy

**API Integrations (Priority Order):**
1. **Seamfix Payroll** (popular in Nigerian SMEs)
2. **WorkPay** (East/West Africa focus)
3. **CloudPay** (multinationals)

**Integration Pattern:**
- OAuth connection for employer authorization
- Webhook for monthly payroll cycle notifications
- BNPL deduction file submission (CSV/API)
- Reconciliation report download

**Fallback:** Manual CSV upload for employers without API-enabled payroll systems

### Compliance & Data Privacy

**NDPR (Nigeria Data Protection Regulation):**
- Data minimization (collect only necessary fields)
- Explicit consent for data processing (BNPL credit checks, employer analytics)
- Right to erasure and data portability
- Privacy policy in plain language
- Data breach notification protocol (<72 hours)

**CBN Regulations (BNPL/Lending):**
- Partner with licensed provider for Phase 1-2
- Apply for Payment Service Provider (PSP) license
- BNPL licensing process (9-12 months) in parallel with Phase 3

**KYC Requirements:**
- BVN (Bank Verification Number) for BNPL eligibility
- NIN (National Identification Number) optional
- Employer email verification for trust layer

### Scalability Considerations

**Current Architecture:** Modular Monolith (all services in single codebase but logically separated)

**Future Microservices (when needed at scale):**
- User Service (authentication, profiles)
- Transaction Service (payments, ledger)
- Credit Service (BNPL scoring, loan management)
- Notification Service (emails, SMS, push)
- Analytics Service (reporting, dashboards)

**Database Sharding:** Partition by employer_id once exceeding 100k active users

**Caching Strategy:**
- Deal feeds cached for 15 minutes (Redis)
- Merchant directory cached for 1 hour
- User session data in Redis (30-day TTL)

## Risks and Assumptions

### Strategic Risks & Mitigation

**RISK 1: Chicken-and-Egg Death Spiral**
- **Scenario:** Can't get merchants without users; can't get users without merchants
- **Warning Signs:** <15% employee activation in first pilot; merchant churn at Month 6
- **Prevention:**
  - Launch with **anchor tenant** (500+ employee employer) signed before merchant outreach
  - Pre-sign **30+ merchants** with non-binding Letters of Intent before employee launch
  - Subsidize initial merchant discounts if needed (â‚¦5M budget)
  - Set 40% activation milestone in first 90 days as success gate
- **Assumption:** Hyperlocal density (10-15 merchants per business district) beats broad shallow coverage

**RISK 2: Regulatory Roadblock (BNPL Licensing)**
- **Scenario:** CBN approval delays 12+ months; competitor captures market while awaiting license
- **Warning Signs:** CBN approval drags past Month 12; competitor announces employer BNPL feature
- **Prevention:**
  - Start regulatory process NOW (Month 0)
  - Launch MVP **without BNPL** first (marketplace + escrow only)
  - **Build proprietary infrastructure** during wait time (Phase 2-3)
  - **No interim partner**: Avoid regulatory grey areas by waiting for full license (Phase 4 launch)
- **Assumption:** Marketplace traction sufficient to sustain growth for 18 months without BNPL revenue

**RISK 3: BNPL Default Crisis**
- **Scenario:** 35% default rate destroys unit economics; merchant payouts delayed; reputation ruined
- **Warning Signs:** Default rate >15% in first 4 months; merchant settlement complaints; negative reviews
- **Prevention:**
  - **Conservative credit limits:** â‚¦5k-â‚¦20k max per employee
  - **Employer payroll deduction required** (contractual agreement with employer)
  - 6-month employment tenure requirement for BNPL eligibility
  - Partner with **credit bureaus** (CRC Credit, FirstCentral) for risk scoring
  - **Reserve fund** (10% of BNPL volume) for merchant payouts in case of defaults
- **Assumption:** Payroll integration achieves >85% collection rate (employees can't easily avoid)

**RISK 4: Escrow Abuse & Fraud**
- **Scenario:** Employees falsely claim dissatisfaction; merchants lose revenue and abandon platform
- **Warning Signs:** 25% dispute rate (vs. 5% expected); top merchants threatening to leave
- **Prevention:**
  - **Evidence-based dispute resolution** (photos, delivery confirmation required)
  - Limit escrow to **first purchase** with each emerging merchant (subsequent purchases instant settlement)
  - **Auto-release after 14 days** with 3-day notification countdown
  - AI **fraud detection** (velocity checks, pattern analysis)
  - **Merchant protection policy:** Frivolous disputes lead to employee account suspension
- **Assumption:** <5% legitimate dispute rate for quality merchants

**RISK 5: Employer Apathy**
- **Scenario:** Employers don't promote internally; usage flatlines at 8%; renewal jeopardized
- **Warning Signs:** Single launch email and no follow-up; no internal champions; HR unresponsive to inquiries
- **Prevention:**
  - **White-glove onboarding:** Dedicated customer success manager per employer
  - **Co-create launch campaign** with HR (ready-made email templates, posters, Slack announcements)
  - **Gamification:** Savings leaderboards visible to employees (social proof drives usage)
  - **Quarterly business reviews** showing ROI (tax savings, employee satisfaction scores)
  - **Incentivized internal champions:** Reward HR with bonus features or credits
- **Assumption:** Employers will promote if shown clear ROI and given easy tools

**RISK 6: Platform Fatigue ("Another App")**
- **Scenario:** Employees ignore yet another benefits platform; activation <20%
- **Warning Signs:** Low click-through rates on launch emails; high app abandonment in first session
- **Mitigation:**
  - **Email-first distribution:** 1-2 curated deals/week to company email (not app-dependent)
  - **PWA (Progressive Web App):** No app store download required; works on low-bandwidth
  - **Employer endorsement:** Launch via company email (trusted sender, not spam)
  - **Instant value:** Show personalized deals immediately upon login (no lengthy onboarding)
- **Assumption:** Email channel + employer trust overcomes app fatigue

**RISK 7: Competitor Copying (Motherboard)**
- **Scenario:** Motherboard adds BNPL and escrow features; neutralizes differentiation
- **Warning Signs:** Competitor funding announcement; feature releases matching Stipends roadmap
- **Mitigation:**
  - **Speed to market:** Launch MVP in 6 months (before competitor can pivot)
  - **Network effects moat:** Employer partnerships harder to replicate than features
  - **Tax optimization positioning:** Requires payroll integration expertise (not trivial to bolt on)
  - **Trust moat:** Multi-layer vetting + escrow is operationally complex (not just feature flag)
- **Assumption:** 6-12 month head start creates defensible employer network
